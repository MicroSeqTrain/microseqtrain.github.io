GalaxyTrakr

GalaxyTrakr is an open source platform for sharing bioinformatics tools and workflows. It allows users to import their own sequencing data or data housed at the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) and run these tools and workflows to clean, analyze, and visualize that data. Many teams of scientists and bioinformaticists are responsible for the tools found on GalaxyTrakr, and references to these creators are usually found at the bottom of an individual tool’s page. Overall maintenance of Galaxy is the responsibility of The Galaxy Team. It was originally created with the support of Penn State, Johns Hopkins, and Oregon Health & Science Universities and many others.

Reminder: If you are using the GalaxyTrakr instance available to GenomeTrakr labs and their collaborators at https://galaxytrakr.org, your data is stored in a public environment and should not contain aspects that are commercially confidential or any personally identifiable information (PII). Keep this in mind even if you do not intend to make your dataset available to other users on the site.


About Us

MicroSeqTrain is a team of public health practitioners and scientists from the US FDA’s Human Foods Program (HFP) and Center for Veterinary Medicine (CVM). Our aim is to help our colleagues in public health across the nation and around the world analyze their sequencing data, even if they have minimal bioinformatics resources at their disposal. Join us here as we continue to add to our collection of available GalaxyTrakr tool tutorials and eventually expand to wet lab sequencing techniques.

If you have any questions or suggestions for the MicroSeqTrain team, please reach out to "microseqtrain@fda.hhs.gov".
