GalaxyTrakr Basics: Troubleshooting

When something goes wrong during one of your GalaxyTrakr tasks, there are some basic steps you should take to see what may have gone wrong. This tutorial_stylesheet will cover those along with who you can reach out to if the problem is with the tool itself or you need more help.


Errors will occassionally occur while using GalaxyTrakr. The first step for figuring out what went wrong will usually be to click on the hyperlink of the step that failed, look for the bug icon near the middle of the tool's card, and clicking on it to view information about what went wrong. Sometimes, this information will point you to your data. Other times, it will be the options you selected for using the tool. Other troubleshooting information may, unfortunately, be rather inscrutable. Do as best you can finding the source of the error, and try the strategies in the Squashing the Bug section to try to eliminate the error. if all else fails, there are references and contacts listed in the Contact section.


  • If your data is simply in the wrong file format for a tool or workflow and you do not have the correct format on hand, there is usually a tool in GalaxyTrakr that can convert it for you. Use the search bar in the tool pane to search [incorrect format] to [correct format], for example, to find such a tool. Run it on your data and try using the tool again.
  • If you have found no problems with your input data and believe the problem lies with how you have used a tool, click on the hyperlink of the tool that failed in your history. There should be a button that looks like a refresh button (arrow pointing in a circle) near where the bug button was. This button will open the tool that was used for this step in the main pane. Re-enter the inputs and options for the tool in the manner you think will lead to success, and restart the tool.
  • In some cases, such as when you want to export a history and only want correct items in it, it may be helpful for you to create a new history and copy only the items from your old history that functioned properly over to it. Then, begin working from the point where the error occurred in your new history.

Sometimes, you may feel like a problem is too big for you to solve on your own or that there is a problem with a tool or GalaxyTrakr itself. In those cases:

  • If you believe the issue is with a tool, you can usually find references, often containing contact information, at the bottom of a tool's page in GalaxyTrakr and here in these MicroSeqTrain tutorials.
  • If you believe the problem lies with how GalaxyTrakr itself is functioning, rather than an individual too, check out the GalaxyTrakr Support Page.
  • You can also reach out to us for assistance at microseqtrain@fda.hhs.gov.
